Last semester(August ~ December,2016), I attended the Machine Learning course taught by Yan Liu at University of Southern California. The experience in this class was awesome and unforgetable, so I plan to write a few posts to share what I learnt in this class.

During last 4 months, we learnt about most of the basic Machine Learning algorithms and methods, including some important and basic methods, such as Maximum Likehood Estimator(MLE), EM algorithm, and basic algorithms, such as KNN, Decision Tree, etc.

This course was very rigorous, we had 6 homeworks and 2 exams, as well as a mini project, which was a competition hold by Toutiao, the competition can be found here. Not only just need to know the concept of those algorithms, we also have to grasp the derivations of the Machine Learning methods, as a CS student with no Statistics background, it is really hard.

Following are the knowledge points of this course:

1. Review of Probability and Statictics
2. Density estimation
3. KNN
4. Decision Tree
5. Naive Bayes
6. Logistic Regression and Multiclass classification
7. Gaussian Discriminat Analysis
8. Linear Regression
9. Bias and Variance
10. SVM
11. Boosting
12. Clustering and EM algorithm
13. Neural network and Deep Learning
14. Dimension reduction
15. Recommender system
16. Hidden Markov Model
17. Reinforcement Learning
